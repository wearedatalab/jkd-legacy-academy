The Lineage

The Legacy
That Lives On.

An unbroken chain — from Bruce Lee, through his first-generation disciples, to today's Kwoon. Every technique, every principle, every breath in the academy traces back to these extraordinary men.

Sijo · The Founder

Bruce Lee

27 NOV 1940 — 20 JUL 1973

In July 1967, Bruce Lee gave a name to the art he had spent his life refining: Jeet Kune Do — the Way of the Intercepting Fist. It was not a style. It was the absence of style — a non-classical method built around honesty of expression and freedom from form.

His philosophy of "using no way as way, having no limitation as limitation" revolutionised martial arts and remains the seed from which modern mixed martial arts grew. More than half a century later, his teachings continue to challenge every generation to strip away the inessential.

The Masters Who Forged the Path

His Direct
Teachers.

Two extraordinary masters personally trained Sigung Ricardo Vargas — and through him, every student in the academy. Each was a direct disciple of Bruce Lee.

Direct Teacher of Sigung Vargas
29 Nov 1936 — 15 Jan 2012
Sifu Jerry Poteet
Hollywood's JKD instructor · Trainer of Jason Scott Lee

A regular student at Bruce Lee's school, Sifu Poteet became a master teacher who shaped world-class JKD practitioners across decades. He famously trained actor Jason Scott Lee for the 1993 film Dragon: The Bruce Lee Story.

His direct mentorship of Sigung Ricardo Vargas anchors the Australian lineage. The Jerry Poteet Jeet Kune Do Association continues his work today.

Direct Teacher of Sigung Vargas
28 Jan 1942 — 30 Mar 2017
Sigung Richard Bustillo
"The Iron Dragon" · Founder of IMB Academy

One of JKD's most influential ambassadors, Sigung Bustillo studied directly under Bruce Lee and founded the International Martial Arts & Boxing Academy in California. His powerful boxing background helped shape the striking aspects of JKD as we know them.

He personally certified Sigung Vargas — making the JKD Legacy Academy in Australia a direct extension of his life's work.

Brothers in the Lineage

The First
Generation.

Five of Bruce Lee's original disciples whose lives, teachings, and influence flow into the same river of tradition that runs through the academy today.

Seattle Lineage
Born 12 Mar 1924
Sifu Taky Kimura
Bruce Lee's closest friend · Best man at his wedding

One of Bruce Lee's earliest students, Sifu Kimura met Bruce in San Francisco in 1959 and became one of his most trusted companions. The bond between them ran far deeper than instruction.

The First Student
15 Oct 1935 — 27 May 2012
Sifu Jesse Glover
Bruce Lee's first student and assistant instructor

A champion judo practitioner when he met Bruce in Seattle in 1959, Sifu Glover became Bruce's very first student — present during the formative years of JKD.

Living Legend
Born 24 Jun 1936
Guro Dan Inosanto
The living embodiment of Bruce Lee's Jeet Kune Do

Widely considered the foremost living authority on JKD, Guro Inosanto trained side-by-side with Bruce Lee and carries the responsibility of preserving and transmitting the art globally.

Sparring Partner
5 Nov 1937 — 24 Nov 2010
Sifu Ted Wong
Bruce Lee's sparring partner and close friend

Sifu Wong met Bruce at a 1967 Gung Fu seminar and became one of his closest students at the Los Angeles school — present during the development of the most refined principles of JKD.

The Master Maker
1916 — 16 Dec 2013
Sifu George Lee
Builder of Bruce Lee's personal training equipment

After witnessing a young Bruce Lee demonstrate in San Francisco's Chinatown, Sifu George Lee approached him and a lifelong friendship was born. Known as "The Master Maker", he hand-built much of the personal training equipment Bruce designed — receiving the philosophical heart of JKD directly from Sijo himself.

The Bridge

From the Source
to Australia.

Sigung Ricardo Vargas is the direct line between today's students and the founders of Jeet Kune Do. Trained and certified by both Sigung Richard Bustillo and Sifu Jerry Poteet, he carries the second-generation lineage onto Australian soil.

Born in Bogotá, Colombia, he cross-trained extensively before immigrating to America in pursuit of authentic JKD instruction. He opened the Adelaide Kwoon in 2011 and now operates the Melbourne headquarters — building Australia's most authentic home for the art.
